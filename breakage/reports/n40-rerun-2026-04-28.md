# n=40 re-runs — 2026-04-28

Tightens two scenarios that were underpowered at the n=20 sample size used in earlier reports. Companion to the [corpus-density sweep](corpus-density-sweep-2026-04-28.md) and the [original falsification test](falsification-test-2026-04-24.md).

## Scope

Two scenarios re-run at n=40 per arm (TEI vs deterministic control):

1. **`cpu-limit-throttling-advocate`** — at n=20 in the falsification test, Δ=+0.091 with t=1.82 (just under the p<0.05 critical value of 2.024). The pre-registered question: would n=40 cross significance, and in which direction?

2. **`replicas-zero-advocate`** — at n=3 in the corpus-seed verification (2026-04-24), TEI scored 0.91 across all 3 reps after seeds were added (vs 0.50 prior). The narrative-arc question: does the "before/after corpus seeding" flip hold up at n=40?

160 runs total (2 scenarios × 2 arms × 40 reps), Sonnet 4.6, ~8.5 hours wall-clock.

## Results

| Scenario | n_tei | μ_tei | σ_tei | n_ctrl | μ_ctrl | σ_ctrl | Δ | t | sig |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| `cpu-limit-throttling-advocate` | 40 | 0.689 | 0.106 | 40 | 0.720 | 0.116 | **−0.031** | −1.25 | ns |
| `replicas-zero-advocate` | 40 | 0.910 | 0.000 | 40 | 0.907 | 0.017 | +0.003 | +1.00 | ns |

## Findings

### `cpu-throttling`: the original near-significance was noise, and the sign flipped

The hypothesis was: at n=40 the +0.091 effect from n=20 would cross to p<0.05. Falsified. At n=40, the point estimate is *negative* (−0.031), and not significant.

This aligns with the corpus-density sweep's finding for the same scenario: cpu-throttling consistently shows negative or near-zero Δ across density tiers (Stream A: −0.041 / −0.005 / −0.068). Stream B at n=40 confirms it's not the underpowered noise the falsification test suggested — it's a genuinely flat-or-slightly-negative effect.

**Combined evidence**: cpu-throttling at three different n=20+ samples (falsification test n=20, density sweep cells with n=20 each, this n=40 re-run) all converge on "retrieval doesn't help here, may slightly hurt." The mechanism explanation from the density sweep applies — the corpus's `resource-limit-misconfiguration` postmortems are predominantly memory-OOM, not CPU-throttling, so retrieval surfaces semantically-near but mechanistically-wrong precedents.

### `replicas-zero`: both arms at ceiling, retrieval doesn't matter

Both arms scored ~0.91 — essentially identical. TEI's standard deviation was 0.000 (every rep landed at exactly 0.91); control had σ=0.017 (one rep dropped to 0.83 in one of 40 reps).

The original n=3 result that motivated picking this scenario (TEI 0.91 / control "much lower based on n=3" prior data) was a small-sample artifact. At n=40, both arms perform near-perfectly. The agent figures out "scale the Deployment back up to the desired replica count" reliably from K8s state alone, without needing retrieval to point the way.

**Implication**: this scenario is too easy at the current model class to discriminate. A future tightening would either pick a harder scenario (where retrieval might help on the margin) or test on a weaker model class (where retrieval might compensate for reduced reasoning).

## What this changes about the published findings

The n=20 falsification test reported `cpu-throttling` Δ=+0.091 with t=1.82 ns and described it as "near-significant." Both density-sweep and Stream B agree the original sign was noise — the underlying effect is small, zero, or slightly negative. Future references to the falsification finding should cite either the density sweep's per-scenario data or this Stream B's n=40 result, *not* the original n=20 number.

The n=3 corpus-seed-verify report (2026-04-24) on `replicas-zero` showed TEI 0.91 vs control 0.50 — a +0.41 swing attributed to corpus seeding. Stream B at n=40 shows control at 0.907 (almost identical to TEI). The +0.41 swing was a small-n artifact of which 3 control reps happened to time out. With n=40 the difference dissolves.

This isn't a falsification of "corpus seeding helps" generally — it's a falsification of the specific replicas-zero claim. The fact that both arms now reach ~0.91 *is* an independent piece of evidence that the corpus seeding from 2026-04-24 worked: the agent reliably reaches the correct fix even when retrieval is off, suggesting the agent's baseline competence on this scenario class is high enough that retrieval is no longer the limiting factor.

## What remains

The corpus-density sweep and this n=40 re-run together establish the publication-quality picture for the three scenarios most-tested in Phase-1:

- `secret-missing-key`: retrieval helps significantly at every density tier (sweep, n=60)
- `cpu-throttling`: retrieval doesn't help, may slightly hurt (sweep + n=40 re-run, n=20+40+20+20 = 100 total)
- `replicas-zero`: too easy to discriminate at current model class (n=40)
- `liveness-probe`: too noisy at the 600s time budget (sweep, n=60, σ ≈ 0.21)

For the next phase (publication, then post-publication):
- `secret-missing-key`-class scenarios are the success case to lead with.
- `cpu-throttling` is the case study for "corpus mechanistic balance matters more than count."
- `replicas-zero` (and likely other "easy" scenarios) need to be excluded or replaced when they ceiling-out.
- `liveness-probe` and other high-variance scenarios need either more reps or a budget/convergence fix before they can contribute signal.

## Reproducibility

```bash
bash breakage/scripts/n40-rerun.sh
```

Per-cell raw scores: `/tmp/n40-rerun-manifest.csv`. Statistical analysis: this report's tables, computed via `python3 /tmp/analyze-n40.py /tmp/n40-rerun-manifest.csv`.
